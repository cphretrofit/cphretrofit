The PAS 2035 Framework

PAS 2035 is the UK standard for retrofitting domestic dwellings. It mandates a "Whole House, Fabric First" approach. This means we prioritize the building envelope before adding expensive heating technologies.

  • Deliver genuine energy and carbon savings
  • Protect occupant health and comfort
  • Avoid unintended consequences (damp, mould, structural damage)

Basic Building Physics

Heat flows from warm to cold. Understanding the three mechanisms of heat loss is crucial for effective retrofit design.

Conduction

Heat flow through solid elements (walls, roofs, floors).

Solution: Insulation materials trap air pockets to slow this transfer. Lower thermal conductivity (Œª) means better resistance.

Convection

Heat loss via air movement (draughts, uncontrolled ventilation).

Solution: Airtightness measures. Sealing gaps around windows, doors, and service penetrations.

Radiation

Heat emitted from warm internal surfaces to colder external ones.

Solution: Low-emissivity (Low-E) coatings on glazing reflect heat back into the room.

PAS 2035 Summary

Under PAS 2035, fabric measures must be assessed as part of a whole dwelling retrofit plan, designed with moisture and ventilation risks in mind, and installed to a high standard of workmanship.

1

Whole House Plan

Assess the dwelling as a system, not individual parts.

2

Risk Management

Design out moisture, condensation, and ventilation issues.

3

Quality Workmanship

Installation standards are critical for performance.

Improving the building fabric is the foundation of safe, effective, and long-lasting domestic retrofit.
