What Is Solar PV?

Understanding the fundamentals of solar photovoltaic technology

‚ö°

Definition

Solar photovoltaic (PV) technology converts sunlight directly into electricity using semiconductor materials. A solar PV array is a group of interconnected solar panels installed together to produce electrical power.

üîç Did You Know?

The term "photovoltaic" comes from the Greek word phos (light) and the unit of electromotive force, the volt. Literally: "light-electricity!"

‚ö° Key Electrical Characteristics

  • Nominal power (Wp): Rated output under Standard Test Conditions (STC)
  • Open-circuit voltage (Voc): Maximum voltage with no load
  • Short-circuit current (Isc): Current with output shorted

Section 4.2

How a Solar PV Array Works

From photons to power: The photovoltaic process

The Photovoltaic Effect

1

Sunlight Hits

Photons strike the solar cell surface

2

Electron Excitation

Photons excite electrons in the semiconductor material

3

Current Generated

An electric current is created and captured

From Sunlight to Usable Electricity

  1. Solar panels produce DC electricity
  2. DC electricity flows through cabling to the inverter
  3. Inverter converts DC to AC electricity
  4. AC electricity is used on-site or exported to the grid

üîß Role of the Inverter

The inverter is the "brain" of the system:

  • DC to AC conversion - Essential for grid compatibility
  • System monitoring - Tracks performance and detects faults
  • Grid synchronization - Matches frequency and phase
  • Safety shutdown - Protects against grid faults

Basic System Performance and Monitoring

Measuring success and maintaining optimal operation

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

üìä Energy Yield (kWh)

Total electrical energy generated by the system over a specific period

‚ö° Specific Yield (kWh/kWp)

Energy produced per kilowatt-peak of installed capacity - allows comparison between systems

üìà Performance Ratio (PR)

Ratio of actual energy output to theoretical maximum, accounting for losses (typically 80-85%)

üßÆ Performance Ratio Formula

PR = (Actual Energy Output) √∑ (Theoretical Energy Output) √ó 100%

A PR of 85% means the system is performing well, accounting for temperature losses, inverter efficiency, cable losses, and soiling.

Health, Safety, and Standards

MCS Overview and regulatory compliance

‚ö° Electrical Safety Critical Warning

DC voltages remain present during daylight hours even when systems are switched off!

  • Always follow safe isolation procedures for AC and DC systems
  • Use appropriate PPE and insulated tools
  • Ensure proper labelling in line with UK regulations

  • Roof access planning and risk assessments mandatory
  • Use of fall protection equipment (harnesses, anchor points)
  • Safe systems of work and method statements
  • Weather considerations (wind, rain, ice)
  • Edge protection and scaffolding where appropriate
