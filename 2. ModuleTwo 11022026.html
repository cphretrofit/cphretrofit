‚ö†Ô∏è The Problem: Retrofit Before PAS

Before PAS standards, retrofit work was often:

Disconnected

One measure at a time without considering the whole house. Insulation installed without ventilation, leading to condensation.

Poorly Planned

No risk assessment. No consideration of how measures interact (e.g., airtightness without ventilation).

Risky

Resulting in:

  • Damp & Mould
  • Overheating
  • Poor Indoor Air Quality
  • Structural Damage
  • Fire Risks
The Solution: PAS 2035 fixes this by mandating a whole-home, risk-managed approach. "You must look at the whole home, assess the risks, and manage the work properly."

üìã What is PAS 2035?

Definition: PAS 2035 is the UK standard that sets out how domestic retrofit projects must be planned, designed, managed, and overseen.

Core Principle: Whole-Dwelling, Risk-Managed

  • You look at the entire home, not just one measure.
  • You understand how changes affect moisture, heat, and air.
  • You manage risk before installing anything.

üõ†Ô∏è What is PAS 2030?

Definition: PAS 2030 is the UK quality standard for carrying out energy efficiency retrofit work in existing homes.

In simple terms: PAS 2030 sets the rules for how retrofit work must be designed, installed, checked, and handed over so it is safe, effective, and high quality.

Mandatory Compliance: If you want to work on government-funded or regulated retrofit schemes (ECO, SHDF, GBIS, etc.), PAS 2030 compliance is mandatory.

What PAS 2030 Covers:

  • Insulation (loft, cavity, solid wall, floor)
  • Windows and doors
  • Heating systems
  • Ventilation
  • Renewable technologies (heat pumps, solar PV)
  • Controls and associated works

üîë 8 Key Principles of PAS 2030

Every installer must understand and apply these principles:

1. Competence is Mandatory

Only qualified people can carry out work. You cannot "have a go." Operatives must be qualified; businesses must be PAS 2030 accredited with a valid TrustMark number.

2. Follow the Design

Install exactly as specified. Correct materials, thicknesses, fixings, and sequencing. No on-site changes without approval.

3. Fabric First

Avoid causing damp, mould, or overheating. Consider how one measure affects the rest of the home (e.g., insulation + ventilation).

4. Quality Standards

Workmanship must be neat, durable, and meet manufacturer instructions and Building Regulations (fire safety, weatherproofing).

5. Testing & Evidence

Work must be inspected, tested, and recorded. Photos, checklists, test results. "If it isn't documented, it didn't happen."

6. Consumer Protection

Clear information to customer, work explained simply, home left safe, warranties provided.

7. Handover & Aftercare

Customer receives operating instructions, maintenance guidance, guarantees, and contact details. Retrofit doesn't end when you leave site.

8. Complaints Process

Clear procedure for complaints, fixing defects, and cooperating with oversight bodies.

üö® Critical Rules to Remember

‚ùå "We always do it this way" is NOT acceptable

Every project is different. The Retrofit Coordinator must review and approve any changes. Risks must be reassessed.

‚ùå Installers cannot "Design on Site"

Changing the design without approval violates PAS 2035. If the design doesn't work, stop and contact the Retrofit Coordinator.

‚ùå Ventilation is NOT Optional

If you make a home more airtight, you MUST assess and upgrade ventilation. This is one of the most critical requirements.

‚úÖ The Golden Rule

PAS 2035 decides WHAT should happen and WHY.
PAS 2030 makes sure it ACTUALLY happens properly.

üß† Knowledge Check 1

Question 1: Which standard covers the installation quality of insulation?

PAS 2030 is the installation standard. It ensures workmanship, competence, and quality on site. PAS 2035 covers the process and design.

Question 2: Who is the single point of accountability for a retrofit project?

The Retrofit Coordinator oversees the entire project, approves designs and changes, and manages risk. Nothing significant happens without their approval.
CPH Retrofit Induction Programme Slide 11

üß† Knowledge Check 2

Question 3: What must happen if an installer needs to change the design on site?

Stop work and get approval. Installers cannot change designs unilaterally. The Retrofit Coordinator must review and approve any changes to ensure risks are managed.

Question 4: Why is ventilation critical in retrofit?

"Build Tight, Ventilate Right." Adding insulation and airtightness without proper ventilation traps moisture, leading to damp, mould, and poor air quality.
