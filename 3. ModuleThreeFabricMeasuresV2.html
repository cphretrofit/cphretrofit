PAS 2035 is the PAS framework for retrofitting domestic dwellings for improved energy efficiency in the UK.

The "Fabric First" Approach

Under PAS 2035, improvements to the building fabric are prioritized before adding heating or renewable technologies. This ensures:

Understanding U-Values ‚ñº

A U-value expresses the rate of heat loss through a building element (W/m¬≤K). Lower is better.

Example: A wall with U-value 0.30 W/m¬≤K loses twice as much heat as one with 0.15 W/m¬≤K for the same temperature difference.

Click each scenario to reveal the recommended material approach:

High Moisture Risk Environment ‚ñº

Recommendation: Wood fibre or mineral wool (vapour open)

These materials allow moisture to escape, reducing interstitial condensation risk in solid wall constructions.

Limited Space Available ‚ñº

Recommendation: Phenolic foam or PIR board

Lower thermal conductivity means thinner boards achieve the same U-value, crucial for internal wall insulation where floor area is precious.

External Wall Insulation (EWI) ‚ñº

Recommendation: EPS or Mineral Wool systems

EPS offers cost-effectiveness for large projects; mineral wool provides better fire performance and breathability.

üè† Loft Insulation (Cold Roof)

Low Risk

Key Installation Requirements

  • Installed between and over ceiling joists to avoid thermal bridging
  • Maintain eaves ventilation (25mm continuous gap minimum)
  • Insulate loft hatches with minimum 100mm insulation
  • Seal services penetrations (lighting, wiring)
  • Do not compress insulation (reduces effectiveness)

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Risks & Controls

Risk: Blocked Ventilation

Can cause condensation, rot in rafters, and mould growth.

Control: Eaves Vents

Install continuous 25mm eaves ventilation paths. Use rafter trays if necessary.

üèöÔ∏è Room in Roof Insulation

High Complexity

Insulation for habitable spaces within roof structures requires careful detailing due to complex junctions at eaves, ridges, and dormers.

Room-in-roof insulation showing rigid boards between and below rafters with ventilation gap

Critical Requirements

  • 50mm minimum ventilation gap above insulation (cross-flow)
  • Insulation between and below rafters (two-layer approach)
  • Vapour control layer on warm side
  • Careful eaves detailing to maintain airflow
‚ö†Ô∏è Overheating Risk: Rooms in roof are prone to summer overheating. PAS 2035 may require overheating risk assessment.

Key Performance Features ‚ñº
  • Low-E Coatings: Reflect heat back into the room
  • Warm Edge Spacers: Reduce thermal bridging at glass edge
  • Insulated Frames: uPVC, timber, or thermally broken aluminium
  • Gas Filling: Argon or Krypton between panes

‚öñÔ∏è Balance Required

While triple glazing offers best thermal performance, consider:

  • Reduced solar gain (winter benefit vs summer disadvantage)
  • Daylight transmission reduction
  • Cost-benefit analysis for specific orientation
  • Background ventilation requirements (trickle vents)
